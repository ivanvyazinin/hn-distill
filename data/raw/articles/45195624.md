# Shocking radar footage shows Hellfire missile fired by US military bounce off UFO over ocean

By

[Shane Galvin](https://nypost.com/author/shane-galvin/)

Published Sep. 9, 2025

Updated Sep. 10, 2025, 6:57 a.m. ET

The House Committee on Restoring Public Trust unveiled a shocking new video that shows a hulking US military weapon bounce off a UFO as it flew over the ocean.

[The wild footage](https://x.com/digijordan/status/1965450199175995504) shared by Rep. Eric Burlison (R-Mo.) during the disclosure hearing on Capitol Hill on Tuesday shows a Hellfire missile fired by a US military drone striking a glowing orb that flew off the coast of Yemen.

 [![Grayscale video still showing a glowing object in the sky.](https://nypost.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/09/washington-dc-house-oversight-committee-111034585.jpg?w=1024) 3](# "Open a slideshow of all 3 article images.")

Radar footage from a US military drone showed the moment a Hellfire missile directly hit a UFO. Reuters

The speeding object was paced by a 100-pound Hellfire air-to-ground round fired by an MQ-9 drone, which made contact and bounced right off, as shown in the radar footage from October 30, 2024.

The footage came from a separate MQ-9 drone, which recorded the orb spinning after the missile made contact and continuing on its way, UFO expert George Knapp said at the hearing.

“There’s a server where there’s a whole bank of these kinds of videos that Congress has not been allowed to see — that the public has not been allowed to see,” Knapp said at the hearing.

“That’s a Hellfire missile smacking into that UFO and just bounc\[ing\] right off, and it kept going,” Knapp said, adding, “What the hell is that?”

Two Air Force veteran whistleblowers also testified at the hearing and shared jarring firsthand accounts of witnessing a football field-size rectangular UFO and a gigantic, glowing red square.

 [![Grayscale video still showing a glowing object in the sky.](https://nypost.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/09/washington-dc-house-oversight-committee-111034588.jpg?w=1024) 3](# "Open a slideshow of all 3 article images.")

The glowing unidentified object continues to fly after being hit by the 100-pound round. Reuters

Former Air Force military police officer Jeffrey Nuccetelli experienced several UAP (unidentified aerial phenomena) incidents while stationed at Vandenberg Air Force Base in California, which is home to the National Missile Defense Project.

“These facilities were vital and they were repeatedly visited by UAP,” Nuccetelli testified under oath.

On October 14, 2003, Boeing contractors reported seeing a massive, glowing red square silently hovering over two missile defense sites before “drifting east” and vanishing behind hills.

Later that same night, Nuccetelli was awakened by frantic calls from other security guards who said a “bright, fast-moving object” was headed right at the base.

“When I arrived, five shaken witnesses described a massive rectangular craft, larger than a football field, that hovered silently for about 45 seconds before shooting away at an incredible speed,” Nuccetelli said.

One week later, security officials declared an emergency as a strange light over the ocean — which they believed to be an unmanned craft — headed for the base.

Before forces arrived, the craft, which descended and briefly hovered close to the base, vanished “instantly” into the night sky, the Air Force veteran cop testified.

Nuccetelli also said he and two friends saw an object that maneuvered “like a butterfly” dancing 200 feet over his house.

 [![Five witnesses being sworn in at a House Oversight Committee hearing on UAP transparency.](https://nypost.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/09/u-s-air-force-veteran-111030585.jpg?w=1024) 3](# "Open a slideshow of all 3 article images.")

Jeffery Nuccetelli, Alexandro Wiggins, George Knapp, Dylan Borland and Joe Spielberg all testified about their own, often firsthand, experiences with UFOs. REUTERS

US Air Force veteran Dylan Borland testified that he personally witnessed a massive, triangular UFO at Langley Air Force Base in Virginia during the summer of 2012.

At about 1:30 a.m., he saw “a 100-foot equilateral triangle take off from near the NASA hangar on the base,” Borland said under oath.

“The craft interfered with my telephone, did not have any sound, and the material … appeared fluid or dynamic,” the airman said.

The object first appeared as a circle and then revealed its true shape as it was closer to Borland, he testified.

“I was under the triangular craft for a few minutes and then it rapidly ascended to commercial jet levels in seconds, displaying zero kinetic disturbance, sound or wind displacement.”

Borland claimed that since he witnessed the out-of-this-world craft, his career has derailed to the point where he was unable to be employed within the intelligence community.

The vet further claimed that the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office had previously misreported events and should be met with skepticism.
