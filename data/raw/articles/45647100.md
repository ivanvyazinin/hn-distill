# An Unexpected Benefit from Quitting Coffee - 10 Months In

Date: 2025-10-15 | [caffeine](/blog/tags/caffeine) | [coffee](/blog/tags/coffee) | [health](/blog/tags/health) | [productivity](/blog/tags/productivity) | [reflect](/blog/tags/reflect) |

I quit coffee at the beginning of the year and shared [how it affected me as a software engineer](https://hamy.xyz/blog/2025-04_i-quit-coffee) and in [my most recent job search](https://hamy.xyz/blog/2025-04_job-search-results).

The primary benefits were:

*   Getting to sleep on time more often and waking up in the mornings feeling rested
*   Don't need caffeine to start doing things (no zombie feel)
*   Less anxiety and easier to enter a focus session

I still feel these benefits are true but I've also discovered a new one, 10 months into quitting coffee.

## The Unexpected Benefit of Quitting Coffee

Over the past 3 months my sleep schedule and quality has taken a severe hit. I had a baby and babies like to wake up every 2-3 hours which eliminates the possibility of a full night's sleep unless you do something like [sleep shifts](https://hamy.xyz/blog/2025-08_newborn-parent-sleep-schedule).

I've also had a few very late nights this past year from birthdays to weddings to traveling with time changes.

It was through these moments of sleep loss that I noticed a pattern.

Back when I was a coffee monster, I would wake up super groggy and basically be a zombie before my first cup. I'd have a bad mood and have a haze over me that basically prevented any kind of nice, productive morning.

After quitting coffee, I'm still tired. But I feel much less of that haze. I can still get up and do things - exercise, write a blog post, do some research. And none of this is predicated on first grabbing a coffee.

Multiple nights of bad sleep are still debilitating, as I found out with the baby, but one night of bad sleep here or there has had much less impact on me than it had before.

## Next

As a caveat, I do still drink caffeine but it's in much smaller doses - an iced tea or decaf coffee. I don't know if I'll ever fully remove it but I've already seen some big benefits from just limiting my caffeine intake.
