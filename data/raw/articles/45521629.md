The Big Take

# OpenAI, Nvidia Fuel $1 Trillion AI Market With Web of Circular Deals

A wave of deals and partnerships are escalating concerns that the trillion-dollar AI boom is being propped up by interconnected business transactions.

By [Emily Forgash](/authors/AW4BeVx3KeQ/emily-forgash) and [Agnee Ghosh](/authors/AXqQdR8XiCM/agnee-ghosh)

October 7, 2025 at 8:00 PM UTC

Updated on

BookmarkSave

Two weeks ago, Nvidia Corp. agreed to [invest as much as $100 billion](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-22/nvidia-to-invest-100-billion-in-openai-in-ai-computing-buildout) in OpenAI to help the leading AI startup fund a data-center buildout so massive it could power a major city. OpenAI in turn committed to filling those sites with millions of Nvidia chips. The arrangement was [promptly criticized](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-23/nvidia-s-massive-openai-deal-fuels-circular-financing-concerns) for its “circular” nature.

This week, undeterred, OpenAI struck a similar deal. The ChatGPT maker on Monday [inked a partnership](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-06/openai-signs-amd-chips-deal-worth-tens-of-billions-of-dollars) with Nvidia rival Advanced Micro Devices Inc. to deploy tens of billions of dollars’ worth of its chips. As part of the tie-up, OpenAI is poised to become one of AMD’s largest shareholders.
