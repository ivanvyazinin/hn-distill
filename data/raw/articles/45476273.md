# The UK Is Still Trying to Backdoor Encryption for Apple Users

DEEPLINKS BLOG

By [Thorin Klosowski](/about/staff/thorin-klosowski-0)

October 1, 2025

![](https://www.eff.org/files/banner_library/OG-Encryption-DefendEncryption.png)

# The UK Is Still Trying to Backdoor Encryption for Apple Users

**Share It** [Share on Twitter](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=The%20UK%20Is%20Still%20Trying%20to%20Backdoor%20Encryption%20for%20Apple%20Users&url=https%3A//www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/10/uk-still-trying-backdoor-encryption-apple-users&via=eff&related=eff "Share on Twitter") [Share on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/share.php?u=https%3A//www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/10/uk-still-trying-backdoor-encryption-apple-users&title=The%20UK%20Is%20Still%20Trying%20to%20Backdoor%20Encryption%20for%20Apple%20Users "Share on Facebook") [Copy link](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/10/uk-still-trying-backdoor-encryption-apple-users "Copy to clipboard")

![](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/OG-Encryption-DefendEncryption.png?itok=bdxk6Y_n)

The [Financial Times reports that](https://www.ft.com/content/d101fd62-14f9-4f51-beff-ea41e8794265?accessToken=zwAGQBl4pP6YkdPRAf1iFPlPUdO-_-pB6HlCZQ.MEYCIQDqJlwcbpQ4rrKlgeSJtbcTgqpW4uTX3yGMCHf2gdS0fQIhAMtU15LHqeHwAXTZ3wWJLDzI72kjsauSKc8OEDpim1Gx&sharetype=gift&token=3767767d-fe68-4911-a97e-c067715e061e) the U.K. is once again demanding that Apple create a backdoor into its encrypted backup services. The only change [since the last time](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/02/uks-demands-apple-break-encryption-emergency-us-all) they demanded this is that the order is allegedly limited to only apply to British users. That doesn’t make it any better.

The demand uses a power called a “Technical Capability Notice” (TCN) in the U.K.’s Investigatory Powers Act. [At the time of its signing](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2016/02/investigatory-powers-bill-and-apple) we noted this law would likely be used to demand Apple spy on its users.

After the U.K. government first issued the TCN in January, [Apple was forced](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/02/cornered-uks-demand-encryption-backdoor-apple-turns-its-strongest-security-setting) to either create a backdoor or block its [Advanced Data Protection](https://ssd.eff.org/module/how-encrypt-your-iphone) feature—which turns on end-to-end encryption for iCloud—for all U.K. users. The company decided to remove the feature in the U.K. instead of creating the backdoor.

The initial order from January targeted the data of _all_ Apple users. In August, the [US claimed the U.K. withdrew the demand](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/us-spy-chief-gabbard-says-uk-agreed-drop-backdoor-mandate-apple-2025-08-19/), but Apple did not re-enable Advanced Data Protection. The new order provides insight into why: the U.K. was just rewriting it to only apply to British users.  

This is still an unsettling overreach that makes U.K. users less safe and less free. As we’ve said [time](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2015/04/clipper-chips-birthday-looking-back-22-years-key-escrow-failures) and [time again](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2019/11/why-adding-client-side-scanning-breaks-end-end-encryption), [any backdoor built for the government](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/10/salt-typhoon-hack-shows-theres-no-security-backdoor-thats-only-good-guys) puts everyone at greater risk of hacking, identity theft, and fraud. It sets a dangerous precedent to demand similar data from other companies, and provides a runway for other authoritarian governments to issue comparable orders. The news of continued server-side access to users' data comes just days after the UK government announced an [intrusive mandatory digital ID scheme](https://www.openrightsgroup.org/press-releases/id-cards-uk-risks-sleeping-walking-into-pre-crime-state/), framed as a measure against illegal migration.

A tribunal hearing was initially set to [take place in January 2026](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c740r0m4mzjo), though it’s currently unclear if that will proceed or if the new order changes the legal process. Apple must continue to refuse these types of backdoors. Breaking end-to-end encryption for one country breaks it for everyone. These repeated attempts to weaken encryption violates [fundamental human rights](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/03/european-court-human-rights-confirms-undermining-encryption-violates-fundamental) and destroys our right to private spaces.
