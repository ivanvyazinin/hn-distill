# AI-Generated “Workslop” Is Destroying Productivity

A confusing contradiction is unfolding in companies embracing generative AI tools: while workers are largely following mandates to embrace the technology, few are seeing it create real value. Consider, for instance, that the number of companies with fully AI-led processes [nearly doubled](https://www.accenture.com/content/dam/accenture/final/accenture-com/document-3/Accenture-Reinventing-Enterprise-Operations-FA-9-25-24.pdf) last year, while AI use has likewise [doubled](https://www.gallup.com/workplace/691643/work-nearly-doubled-two-years.aspx) at work since 2023. Yet a [recent report](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/ai_report_2025.pdf) from the MIT Media Lab found that 95% of organizations see no measurable return on their investment in these technologies. So much activity, so much enthusiasm, so little return. Why?

KN

[Kate Niederhoffer](/search?term=Kate%20Niederhoffer&amp;search_type=search-all) is VP of BetterUp Labs and a social psychologist. Her research explores the role of AI in workplace adoption, human development, and interpersonal communication.

GK

[Gabriella Rosen Kellerman](/search?term=Gabriella%20Rosen%20Kellerman&amp;search_type=search-all) is a physician, the chief product officer, and the chief innovation officer at BetterUp, a coaching platform in San Francisco.

AL

[Angela Lee](/search?term=Angela%20Lee&amp;search_type=search-all) is a PhD Candidate in the Department of Communication at Stanford University. Her work focuses on psychological mechanisms that shape how social technologies influence our health, well-being, and development.

AL

[Alex Liebscher](/search?term=Alex%20Liebscher&amp;search_type=search-all) is a Research Scientist at BetterUp Labs. His research focuses on understanding how we interact with digital technology, plus its impact on our relationships to each other.

KR

[Kristina Rapuano](/search?term=Kristina%20Rapuano&amp;search_type=search-all) is a Research Scientist at BetterUp Labs. Her research explores the psychology at the intersection of technological innovation and personal development.

JH

[Jeffrey T. Hancock](/search?term=Jeffrey%20T.%20Hancock&amp;search_type=search-all) is the Harry and Norman Chandler Professor of Communication at Stanford University. He is also the Founding Director of the Stanford Social Media Lab and Director of the Cyber Policy Center at Stanford. His research focuses on psychological aspects of technology use, including AI and social media.
