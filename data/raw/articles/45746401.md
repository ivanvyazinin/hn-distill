# Grammarly rebrands to ‘Superhuman,’ launches a new AI assistant

[Ivan Mehta](https://techcrunch.com/author/ivan-mehta/)

6:00 AM PDT · October 29, 2025

[](https://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftechcrunch.com%2F2025%2F10%2F29%2Fgrammarly-rebrands-to-superhuman-launches-a-new-ai-assistant%2F)[](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?url=https%3A%2F%2Ftechcrunch.com%2F2025%2F10%2F29%2Fgrammarly-rebrands-to-superhuman-launches-a-new-ai-assistant%2F&text=Grammarly+rebrands+to+%E2%80%98Superhuman%2C%E2%80%99+launches+a+new+AI+assistant&via=techcrunch)[](https://www.linkedin.com/shareArticle?url=https%3A%2F%2Ftechcrunch.com%2F2025%2F10%2F29%2Fgrammarly-rebrands-to-superhuman-launches-a-new-ai-assistant%2F&title=Grammarly+rebrands+to+%E2%80%98Superhuman%2C%E2%80%99+launches+a+new+AI+assistant&summary=Grammarly+is+renaming+itself+as+Superhuman+after+acquiring+the+Superhuman+email+client+in+July.&mini=1&source=TechCrunch)[](https://www.reddit.com/submit?url=https%3A%2F%2Ftechcrunch.com%2F2025%2F10%2F29%2Fgrammarly-rebrands-to-superhuman-launches-a-new-ai-assistant%2F&title=Grammarly+rebrands+to+%E2%80%98Superhuman%2C%E2%80%99+launches+a+new+AI+assistant)[](mailto:?subject=Grammarly+rebrands+to+%E2%80%98Superhuman%2C%E2%80%99+launches+a+new+AI+assistant&body=Article%3A+https%3A%2F%2Ftechcrunch.com%2F2025%2F10%2F29%2Fgrammarly-rebrands-to-superhuman-launches-a-new-ai-assistant%2F)[](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/29/grammarly-rebrands-to-superhuman-launches-a-new-ai-assistant/)

Typically, when a company acquires another, it will absorb the new company’s branding or integrate it with its own identity. Grammarly is doing something different: After [acquiring email client Superhuman in July](https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/01/grammarly-acquires-ai-email-client-superhuman/), the company is renaming itself “Superhuman.”

Despite the branding change, Grammarly, the product, will continue to be known as it has. However, the company says it is thinking about rebranding products like Coda, [a productivity platform it acquired last year](https://techcrunch.com/2024/12/17/grammarly-acquires-productivity-startup-coda-brings-on-new-ceo/), in the long run.

The company is also launching an AI assistant called Superhuman Go that’s built into Grammarly’s existing extension. The assistant can provide writing suggestions, give feedback on emails, and you can even connect it with other apps like Jira, Gmail, Google Drive, and Google Calendar to arm it with more context. The assistant can use these connections to do tasks like logging tickets or fetching your availability when you’re scheduling a meeting.

![](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/GIF_Superhuman-Go-use-case.gif?w=680)

**Image Credits:**Superhuman

Superhuman said it plans to add functionality to enable the assistant to fetch data from sources like CRMs and internal systems to suggest changes to your emails.

Users can try Superhuman Go by turning on a toggle in the Grammarly extension, which will let them connect it to different apps. Users can also try out different agents in the company’s agent store, which include a [plagiarism checker and a proofreader](https://techcrunch.com/2025/08/18/grammarly-gets-a-design-overhaul-multiple-ai-features/), launched in August.

All Grammarly users can try out Superhuman Go right now, though the company is also selling product bundles. Its Pro subscription plan will cost $12 per month (billed annually) and will enable grammar and tone support in multiple languages. The Business plan will cost $33 per month (billed annually) and will give users access to Superhuman Mail.

![](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Static_Superhuman-Go-2.jpeg?w=680)

**Image Credits:**Superhuman

Superhuman said it also wants to add more AI-powered features to the Coda document suite and Superhuman email clients, such as fetching details from external and internal sources to create additional details in documents and email drafts automatically.
