# U.S. Tech Layoffs Hit Two-Decade High in October

by [Frank Ell](https://thefivepost.com/author/frank/) November 9, 2025

by [Frank Ell](https://thefivepost.com/author/frank/) November 9, 2025 0 comments

[![layoffs](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201500%20843'%3E%3C/svg%3E "layoffs")](https://thefivepost.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/layoffs.jpeg)

_6_

October 2025 brought a sharp and unsettling turn in the U.S. labour market: employers announced **153,074 job cuts**, the highest for any October in more than two decades. The data from consulting firm [_Challenger, Gray & Christmas_](https://www.challengergray.com/blog/october-challenger-report-153074-job-cuts-on-cost-cutting-ai/) reveals that the wave of reductions is broadening beyond isolated sectors, signalling deeper structural shifts.

One of the standout features is the surge in the technology sector. Tech companies reported **33,281 job-cut announcements** in October alone — up sharply from just 5,639 in September. Year-to-date through October, tech firms had a combined **141,159 job cuts**, representing roughly a 17 % increase compared to the same period in 2024.

Several drivers are converging to produce this uptick. Cost-cutting is cited as a leading reason, alongside automation and the rapid deployment of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies. Firms are restructuring amid slower consumer demand, rising input costs and the need to adapt to next-generation technologies. For workers, this means that the low-firing environment of recent years is giving way to a more volatile job market.

The implications extend beyond individuals. For digital publishers, blogs and content-platforms like ours, a pullback in workforce and corporate spending may translate into changes in advertising budgets, affiliate-marketing partnerships and content demand. Strategically, this is a moment to focus on stories such as: how workers can pivot their careers, the ripple effects on freelancers and agencies, and how companies are managing the transition to AI-enabled workflows.

In short: the October spike in layoffs is more than a statistical blip—it reflects a shifting employment landscape, one in which technology firms once seen as growth engines are recalibrating and reshaping their workforces. For creators, entrepreneurs and professionals alike, adaptation is no longer optional.
