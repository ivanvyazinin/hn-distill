"The staff ate it later" as shown on screen

"**The staff ate it later**" ([Japanese](/wiki/Japanese_language "Japanese language"): この後、スタッフが美味しくいただきました, [romanized](/wiki/Romanization_of_Japanese "Romanization of Japanese"): _Kono ato, sutaffu ga oishiku itadakimashita_) is a caption shown on screen when food appears in a [Japanese TV](/wiki/Television_in_Japan "Television in Japan") program to indicate that it was not thrown away after filming. Some question the authenticity of this statement, and others believe this caption lowers the quality of TV programs.

## First appearance

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_staff_ate_it_later&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: First appearance")\]

It is thought TV stations first began showing the caption to protect themselves against complaints from viewers who disliked food being handled without consideration in TV [variety shows](/wiki/Variety_show "Variety show").[\[1\]](#cite_note-Furuyu_2017-1) It is uncertain when this note was first used, but TV producer [Kenji Suga](/w/index.php?title=Kenji_Suga&action=edit&redlink=1 "Kenji Suga (page does not exist)") \[[ja](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%8F%85%E8%B3%A2%E6%B2%BB "ja:菅賢治")\] stated viewers complained about the waste of food when a performance using small watermelons was broadcast in _[Downtown no Gaki no Tsukai ya Arahende!!](/wiki/Downtown_no_Gaki_no_Tsukai_ya_Arahende!! "Downtown no Gaki no Tsukai ya Arahende!!")_ on [Nippon TV](/wiki/Nippon_TV "Nippon TV"). The TV station then showed this note on screen the following year in response.[\[2\]](#cite_note-Wake_2016-2)

## Authenticity

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_staff_ate_it_later&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Authenticity")\]

There are various claims as to whether or not staff actually eat the food that appears in the programs.[\[1\]](#cite_note-Furuyu_2017-1)[\[3\]](#cite_note-Kitano_2017-3)[\[4\]](#cite_note-AOL_2014-4)

### Supporting Reports

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_staff_ate_it_later&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Supporting Reports")\]

According to [AOL](/wiki/AOL "AOL") News in 2014, the crew on one information program testified: "It's sometimes impossible for the reporter to eat all the food provided by the restaurant. The reporter is told not to eat it all, but the crew will eat the rest out of consideration and a feeling of obligation towards the restaurant."[\[4\]](#cite_note-AOL_2014-4)

Food comic artist [Raswell Hosoki](/w/index.php?title=Raswell_Hosoki&action=edit&redlink=1 "Raswell Hosoki (page does not exist)") \[[ja](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%A9%E3%82%BA%E3%82%A6%E3%82%A7%E3%83%AB%E7%B4%B0%E6%9C%A8 "ja:ラズウェル細木")\] claimed in "Meshizanmai Furusatonoaji" (Meshizanmai Taste of Hometown), that the note is true. [Eriko Miyazaki](/w/index.php?title=Eriko_Miyazaki&action=edit&redlink=1 "Eriko Miyazaki (page does not exist)") \[[ja](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%AE%AE%E8%84%87%E7%90%86%E6%81%B5%E5%AD%90 "ja:宮脇理恵子")\], a reporter and TV personality for food shows, also claims the note is true and has stated: "The crew eats the rest of the food, at least at the shows I appear in."[\[5\]](#cite_note-5)

In January 2018, [Miwa Asao](/wiki/Miwa_Asao "Miwa Asao"), former professional beach volleyball player and TV personality, posted photos on her blog of staff eating food after recording "[Saturday Night! Otona na TV](/w/index.php?title=Saturday_Night!_Otona_na_TV&action=edit&redlink=1 "Saturday Night! Otona na TV (page does not exist)") \[[ja](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%9C%9F%E6%9B%9C%E3%81%AE%E5%A4%9C%E3%81%AF!%E3%81%8A%E3%81%A8%E3%81%AA%E3%81%AA%E3%83%86%E3%83%AC%E3%83%93 "ja:土曜の夜は!おとななテレビ")\]". She wrote: "This is an on-site photo. The staff ate the rest of the food."[\[6\]](#cite_note-:4-6)

### Refuting Reports

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_staff_ate_it_later&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Refuting Reports")\]

[Hitoshi Matsumoto](/wiki/Hitoshi_Matsumoto "Hitoshi Matsumoto"), a comedian and TV host, was asked by sociologist [Noritoshi Furuichi](/wiki/Noritoshi_Furuichi "Noritoshi Furuichi") about this note in 2014 during the "[Wide na Show](/w/index.php?title=Waido_na_Show&action=edit&redlink=1 "Waido na Show (page does not exist)") \[[ja](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%AF%E3%82%A4%E3%83%89%E3%83%8A%E3%82%B7%E3%83%A7%E3%83%BC "ja:ワイドナショー")\]" ([Fuji Television](/wiki/Fuji_TV "Fuji TV")). He said: "To be honest, I've never seen the crew eat the food. But that just means I haven't seen it. They might've eaten it."[\[7\]](#cite_note-7)

[Takeshi Kitano](/wiki/Takeshi_Kitano "Takeshi Kitano") (also known as Beat Takeshi), Japanese comedian, actor, and filmmaker, referred to an instance where cake was smeared on the floor and said in his book _Bakaron_: "Liars. Who's going to enjoy cake they splattered all over the floor?"[\[3\]](#cite_note-Kitano_2017-3) Commentator [Tsunehira Furuya](/wiki/Tsunehira_Furuya "Tsunehira Furuya") also stated that the food featured in the show is not eaten by the staff later and is instead simply thrown into garbage bags.[\[1\]](#cite_note-Furuyu_2017-1)

## Reception

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_staff_ate_it_later&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Reception")\]

Commentator Tetsuya Uetaki has commented on displaying the note, saying: "Producers have become more aware as viewers have become more critical after issues such as the _Aru Aru Mondai_ (a natto shortage caused by a program claiming eating natto would make people lose weight), and it's fine as one method for dealing with that." However, Uetaki went on to say: "This shifts responsibility onto the viewers. We can't let it end as simply an empty concession. I want to see variety shows strive to properly handle information and properly put the show together, from the moment they start building it."[\[8\]](#cite_note-8)

Broadcast writer [Sotani](/w/index.php?title=Sotani_\(broadcast_writer\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Sotani (broadcast writer) (page does not exist)") \[[ja](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%81%9D%E3%83%BC%E3%81%9F%E3%81%AB "ja:そーたに")\] commented on the fact that production teams have become more sensitive to this in programs and have begun displaying such notes as an attempt to preempt criticism. He claims this sort of extreme self regulation risks leading to a decline.[\[9\]](#cite_note-9) TV producer [Kenji Suga](/w/index.php?title=Kenji_Suga&action=edit&redlink=1 "Kenji Suga (page does not exist)") \[[ja](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%8F%85%E8%B3%A2%E6%B2%BB "ja:菅賢治")\] claims it is necessary for programs to be disconnected from real life and society, to be "dumb and idiotic" to produce laughs.[\[2\]](#cite_note-Wake_2016-2)

Columnist [Takashi Matsuo](/wiki/Takashi_Matsuo_\(actor,_born_1960\) "Takashi Matsuo (actor, born 1960)") argues that adults, not TV shows, should teach children the ethics surrounding the importance of food. He also argues that if a parent is uncomfortable with what a comedian expresses on TV, the right course of action would be to change the channel or turn off the TV, not send a complaint to the TV station.[\[10\]](#cite_note-:5-10) Matsuo also points out the inconsistency that "the staff ate it later" caption is not displayed when large numbers of tomatoes are thrown at the festival of [Tomatina](/wiki/La_Tomatina "La Tomatina") in Spain or when athletes spray each other with champagne in celebration of a victory.[\[10\]](#cite_note-:5-10)
