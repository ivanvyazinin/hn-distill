# I turned the Lego Game Boy into a working Game Boy part. 1

![Lego Game Boy that actually works](https://blog.nataliethenerd.com/content/images/2025/10/DSCF4479.jpg)

Through my documentation of Game Boy boards, I have drawn up schematics of each device. I know them pretty well. Check out my board scan wiki [https://wiki.nataliethenerd.com/](https://wiki.nataliethenerd.com/?ref=blog.nataliethenerd.com)

I jokingly made this tweet when the kit was announced, but decided to actually do it.

I know from experience of routing Game Boy CPU PCBs that there isn't much to it. There's the RAM, CPU, some decoupling capacitors and power regulation.  
  
Note: I went with the MGB (Pocket) CPU rather than DMG for a couple of reasons.

*   They are pretty much the same
*   I have more of them
*   They are cheaper and easier to get. This opens up the project to more people

The DMG CPU has external VRAM, the MGB CPU has internal VRAM and in a very space conscious build that was the biggest factor.

## Pre Planning

I only had the press pictures to work off. I used the dimensions to scale the image on my PC and from that I got measurements for the screen inserts; since that's where I plan to put the Game Boy.

![](https://blog.nataliethenerd.com/content/images/2025/10/72046_WEB_SEC05_en-gb.webp)

![](https://blog.nataliethenerd.com/content/images/2025/10/72046_Lifestyle_Envr_05_en-gb.webp)

Source: [https://www.lego.com/en-au/product/game-boy-72046](https://www.lego.com/en-au/product/game-boy-72046?ref=blog.nataliethenerd.com)

I incorporated the power circuit I use for my [Safer Charger boards](https://nataliethenerd.com/products/safer-charge-dc?ref=blog.nataliethenerd.com), changed the power switch to a soft latching power button, added pin outs for the button matrix and audio.

![](https://blog.nataliethenerd.com/content/images/2025/10/Screenshot-2025-07-31-222906.png)

![](https://blog.nataliethenerd.com/content/images/2025/10/Screenshot-2025-07-31-222621.png)

KiCad 3D renders of the PCB

I didn't really know what the buttons on the Lego would be like, but the fact that they could be pressed was enough for me to know I could implement them. At the moment I have them wired up to custom 3D printed \*toy brick\* parts. Same with the USB C
