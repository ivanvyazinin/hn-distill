## Sizes

[top](#top)

Basically there are four different types or sizes of floppy disks / diskettes:

![diskette types](diskettes_02.jpg)

Diskettes

1.  8 inch floppy disk

*   a.k.a -> FLOPPY DISK
*   203 mm (8") x 203 mm

3.  5.25 inch floppy disk

*   a.k.a -> MINI DISKETTE, MINI DISK, MINI FLOPPY
*   133 mm (5.25") x 133 mm

5.  3.5 inch floppy disk

*   a.k.a -> MICRO DISKETTE, MICRO DISK, MICRO FLOPPY
*   90 mm (3.5") x 93 mm

7.  3 inch floppy disk

*   a.k.a -> COMPACT FLOPPY DISK
*   80 mm (3") x 100 mm

The original name FLOPPY DISK was later simply adopted with the advent of the IBM PC and XT.

## Hard or Soft

[top](#top)

In addition to size, there is also a distinction between soft- and hard-sectored disks. The 3.5" and 3" floppy disks are all soft-sectored. Hard-sectored floppy disks were never used in IBM-compatible computers.

Here you can see an example of a [8 inch floppy disks](../8inch/ss-ds-flippy.htm#hard) with 32 sector holes.

![diskette types](diskette-tytes.png)

Diskette Types / 8 inch & 5,25 inch

## Write Protect/Enable Notch

[top](#top)

### 8 inch

... floppy disks can only be written to when the write protect notch is COVERED.

### 5.25 inch

... floppy disks can only be written to when the write ENABLE notch is UNCOVERED.

## Examples

[top](#top)

### Hard-sectored

Because hard-sectored 5.25 inch floppy disks are very difficult to find/buy today, Mike Douglas invented/constructed the so-called [Virtual Sector Generator (VSG)](https://deramp.com/vsg.html "Virtual Sector Generator").

#### 5.25 inch / 10 Sector Holes

Floppy disks with 16 sector holes are not often found, but those with 10 holes are extremely rare. These disks were used in the following computer systems:

*   [Heathkit H89](https://heathkit.garlanger.com/diskformats/ "Heathkit disk formats")
*   [North Star Horizon](https://deramp.com/northstar.html "North Star Horizon")
