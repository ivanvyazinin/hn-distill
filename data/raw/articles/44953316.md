# [Emacs as your video-trimming tool](/emacs-as-your-video-trimming-tool)

Marcin ‘mbork’ Borkowski has a nice post showing us how he [trims video clips from our beloved editor](https://mbork.pl/2025-08-18_Cutting_clips_from_videos_with_Emacs). Trimming clips is something I do from time, specially when posting a screencast of sorts. Since I don't need much, I typically resort to QuickTime Player's trimming functionality that ships with macOS. While it does the job, ever since I [added a "graphical" seeker to Ready Player Mode](seek-and-you-shall-find), I had been meaning to build a simple video trimming tool of sorts. Marcin's post was just about the right nudge I needed to also give this a go, yielding [video-trimmer-mode](https://github.com/xenodium/dotsies/blob/main/emacs/ar/video-trimmer.el).

![](https://xenodium.github.io/images/emacs-as-your-video-trimming-tool/trim.gif)

The solution relies on [ffmpeg](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9kaIXkImCAM) to do the heavy lifting and is roughly 300 lines of code. I was going to share the entire snippet in this post, though may as well point you to its [location in my Emacs config repo](https://github.com/xenodium/dotsies/blob/main/emacs/ar/video-trimmer.el). I'm likely to tweak it, so you may as well take a look at its latest incarnation.
