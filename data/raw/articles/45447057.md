The **Old Stockholm telephone tower** ([Swedish](/wiki/Swedish_language "Swedish language"): _Telefontornet_) was a metallic structure built to connect approximately 5,500 [telephone](/wiki/Telephone "Telephone") lines in the Swedish capital of [Stockholm](/wiki/Stockholm "Stockholm"). Constructed in 1887, the tower was used until 1913. It was damaged by a fire in 1952 and demolished the following year.

## History

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Old_Stockholm_telephone_tower&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

In 1887, Stockholms Allmänna Telefon AB ordered the construction of a tower allowing the connection of about 5,500 overhead telephone lines. The quadrangular metallic structure was 80 metres tall and soon fell out of favour with the local population. The company requested the architect [Fritz Eckert](/wiki/Fritz_Eckert "Fritz Eckert") to carry out embellishment work, which was when the four turrets were added.

The tower was quickly made obsolete as telephone companies began using [underground cables](/wiki/Undergrounding "Undergrounding") in urban areas. In 1913, underground cabling for telephones was fully completed and the tower no longer served its original purpose. After 1939 it carried advertising for [Nordiska Kompaniet](/wiki/Nordiska_Kompaniet "Nordiska Kompaniet"). On 23 July 1952 a fire weakened the structure, resulting in its demolition in 1953 on safety grounds.[\[1\]](#cite_note-1)
