**_Sütterlinschrift_** (German pronunciation: [\[ˈzʏtɐliːnˌʃʁɪft\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German"), "_Sütterlin_ script") is the last widely used form of _[Kurrent](/wiki/Kurrent "Kurrent")_, the historical form of [German](/wiki/German_language "German language") [handwriting script](/wiki/Handwriting_script "Handwriting script") that evolved alongside German [blackletter](/wiki/Blackletter "Blackletter") (most notably __[Fraktur](/wiki/Fraktur "Fraktur")__) typefaces. Graphic artist [Ludwig Sütterlin](/wiki/Ludwig_S%C3%BCtterlin "Ludwig Sütterlin") was commissioned by the [Prussian](/wiki/Prussia "Prussia") Ministry of Science, Art and Culture (__Preußisches Ministerium für Wissenschaft, Kunst und Volksbildung__) to create a modern handwriting script in 1911. His handwriting scheme gradually replaced the older [cursive](/wiki/Cursive "Cursive") scripts that had developed in the 16th century at the same time that letters in books had developed into [Fraktur](/wiki/Fraktur "Fraktur"). The name __Sütterlin__ is nowadays often used to refer to several similar varieties of old German handwriting, but Sütterlin's own script was taught only from 1915 to 1941 in all German schools.

## History

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=S%C3%BCtterlin&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/%C3%9Cbungen_in_deutscher_Schrift_in_einem_Schulheft_des_ersten_Schuljahres_%281953%29.jpg/250px-%C3%9Cbungen_in_deutscher_Schrift_in_einem_Schulheft_des_ersten_Schuljahres_%281953%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:%C3%9Cbungen_in_deutscher_Schrift_in_einem_Schulheft_des_ersten_Schuljahres_\(1953\).jpg)

First exercises in Sütterlin in a school notebook (1953)

The ministry had asked for "modern" handwriting scripts to be used in offices and to be taught in school. Sütterlin created two scripts in parallel with the two typefaces that were in use (see [Antiqua–Fraktur dispute](/wiki/Antiqua%E2%80%93Fraktur_dispute "Antiqua–Fraktur dispute")). The _Sütterlin_ scripts were introduced in Prussia in 1915, and from the 1920s onwards, they began to replace the relatively similar old German handwriting (_[Kurrent](/wiki/Kurrent "Kurrent")_) in schools. In 1935, the _Sütterlin_ style officially became the only German script taught in schools.

In 1941, [the Nazi Party banned all "broken" (_fraktur_, blackletter) typefaces](/wiki/Antiqua%E2%80%93Fraktur_dispute#Nazi_period "Antiqua–Fraktur dispute"), including _Sütterlin_, and replaced them with Italian-style lettering, such as the [Antiqua typeface class](/wiki/Antiqua_\(typeface_class\) "Antiqua (typeface class)"). From the academic year 1941/42 onwards, only the so-called __Normalschrift__ ("normal script"), which had hitherto been taught alongside _Sütterlin_ under the name of "Latin script", was allowed to be used and taught. However, many German speakers who had been brought up with that writing system continued to use it well into the postwar period.

_Sütterlin_ continued to be taught in some German schools until the 1970s but no longer as the primary script.\[_[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

## Characteristics

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=S%C3%BCtterlin&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Characteristics")\]

_Sütterlin_ is based on older German handwriting, which is a handwriting form of the [Blackletter](/wiki/Blackletter "Blackletter") scripts such as _[Fraktur](/wiki/Fraktur_\(typeface\) "Fraktur (typeface)")_ and _[Schwabacher](/wiki/Schwabacher "Schwabacher")_, the German print scripts used at the same time.

It includes the [long s (ſ)](/wiki/Long_s "Long s") as well as several standard [ligatures](/wiki/Ligature_\(typography\) "Ligature (typography)") such as ﬀ (f-f), ﬅ (ſ-t), ﬆ (s-t), and [ß](/wiki/%C3%9F "ß") (ſ-z or ſ-s).

Because of their distinctiveness, _Sütterlin_ letters can be used on the blackboard for certain [mathematical symbols](/wiki/Mathematical_symbol "Mathematical symbol") that are represented by _Fraktur_ letters in print. The lower-case d in _Kurrent_ and _Sütterlin_ is used in [proofreading](/wiki/Proofreading "Proofreading") for __[deleatur](/wiki/Deleatur "Deleatur")__ ("let it be deleted").

The Sütterlin lower-case 'e' contains two vertical bars close together, in which the origin of the [umlaut](/wiki/Umlaut_\(diacritic\) "Umlaut (diacritic)") [diacritic](/wiki/Diacritic "Diacritic") (¨) from a small 'e' written above the modified vowel can be seen.
