The1953 _The Magnetic Monster_ film features a 511 oscilloscope which is only shown briefly. This film was the first in Ivan Tors' "_Office of Scientific Investigation_" (OSI) trilogy and is the earliest film we know of which features a Tektronix product.

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/The-Magnetic-Monster1.jpg)

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/The-Magnetic-Monster2.jpg)

* * *

_The Magnetic Monster_ was followed in Ivan Tors' _Office of Scientific Investigation_ (OSI) trilogy by _Riders to the Stars_ (1954) and _Gog_ (1954). _Gog_ features a 1952 Type 511A oscilloscope (based on the top louvers). A viewing of _Riders to the Stars_ showed a couple of scenes with non-Tektronix oscilloscopes.

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/gog.jpg)

* * *

This 1955 RKO Pictures _The Future Is Now_ highlights some of the research developments that may become products in the near future. This particular scene shows Type 524AD oscilloscope with a color video tape recorder that uses 1/2" magnetic tape operated at 10 fps (feet per second).  Note that Ampex brought out the first commercial video tape recorder, the VRX-1000, in 1956. They abandoned this high speed linear approach and instead used 2" tape with a helical scan.

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/The-Future-Is-Now-1955.jpg)

The 1976 British series _Children of the Stones_ features a Telequipment oscilloscope. Telequipment was a low cost oscilloscope manufacture located in Hoddesdon, UK, and their equipment was primarily aimed at the television and repair service industry. Tektronix purchased them in 1967 for a low cost entry and phased the brand out by the late 1970s.  This D65 or D66 oscilloscope was introduced in the early 1970s and appears towards the end of episode 7.

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Children-Of-The-Stones1.jpg)

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Children-Of-The-Stones2.jpg) ![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Children-Of-The-Stones3.jpg)

This YouTube video of the scene when the Death Star destroys Alderaan shows the Model 1600 Switcher in action.

The 1977 Season 5 Episode 1 of the TV Series The Six Million Dollar Man features a T922/T932 oscilloscope in a close-up of some kind of cutting edge aircraft radar-invisibility invention. The T900 series of oscilloscopes were affectionately called "Hoovers" given their similarity in appearance to the canister vacuum cleaner.

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/6M-Man-S5E11-1977.jpg)

The 1979 film _Escape From Alcatraz_, featuring Clint Eastwood, shows a Tektronix T922 oscilloscope as part of a walk through detector.  This film is about the 1962 escape which precedes the introduction of the T922 by some 14 years.  Apparently that Time Travelers movie had some validity.

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/Escape-From-Alcatraz1.jpg)

The logo on the T922 has been replaced with one that looks like "Vectrex".

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/Escape-From-Alcatraz2.jpg)

The 1983 movie _Brainstorm_ shows several Tektronix products at about 98 minutes. We think the mainframe might be a 7603. The two plug-ins in the Type 200 Scope-Mobile are a 1S1 (left) and a Type Q (right) which of course don't go with the mainframe.

![](https://vintagetek.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/03/Brainstorm.jpg)
