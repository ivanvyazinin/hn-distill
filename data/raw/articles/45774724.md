notes  
on 90h

XCHG   AX,  AX – classic 16-bit **NOP** (8086)

If CPUID does indicate support, then a **REP** prefix will apply **PAUSE** semantics to a **NOP**.

The blame for that "special treatment" of PM64 O32 NOP – effectively D64 – falls on me.  
   
I did spot that zeroing the upper 32 bits of RAX leads to undesired non-NOP behavior, but  
failed to convince our team in 2001 to use one of the freed up 1-byte opcodes (a la UDB).

XCHG eAX,eAX – wider 32-bit **NOP** (80386)

XCHG  rAX, rAX – wider 64-bit **NOP** (x86-64)

In PM64 with O32, XCHG EAX,EAX must not zero the upper 32 bits of RAX. Thus it is **D64**.

XCHG    r**N**, rAX (**N**\=8

,16,24) is **not** a NOP!
