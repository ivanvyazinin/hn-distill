## How Can You Pause AI? Steps to Stop AI Systems

If you are concerned about AI’s implications on your digital privacy, you can choose to pause AI by adjusting privacy settings, disabling AI features in apps, or requesting data removal from companies.

Many communities have been voicing their concerns and working on ways to [pause AI](https://pauseai.info/proposal) until they become safer to use.

### 1\. Stop AI by Adjusting Privacy Settings

In order to pause AI, you can start by reviewing the settings of platforms you frequently use. This allows you to control the amount of data AI systems collect.

Go to the apps, websites and social media platforms you normally visit, and make sure to modify any AI-related privacy settings.

For example, you can disable Ad Personalization within your Google account.

![stop AI](https://againstdata.s3.eu-central-1.amazonaws.com/upload/Us1j6OIsGMpcpev3z6zvMywMCLWwBTIAnnqqTKYi.jpg)

Login to your Google account > Click on your Profile Picture > Select “Manage Your Google Account” > Go to “Data and Privacy” > Adjust your ad preferences.

### 2\. Disable Artificial Intelligence Features in Apps

Another way to pause AI is to ensure that AI-powered functionalities are turned off in the apps you use. These features include AI recommendations, personalized content, voice assistants and automated actions.

One example to do this is by turning off YouTube recommendations to disable AI from tracking your views and giving you recommendations.

![turn off artificial intelligence](https://againstdata.s3.eu-central-1.amazonaws.com/upload/NttdFaceCISkrUI85Cvb8nV8ACBi0ACnEEzwDb8G.jpg)

Login to your YouTube account > Click on your Profile Picture > Select “Your Data in YouTube” > Scroll down to YouTube controls → Turn Off “YouTube Watch History” and “YouTube Search History”.

### 3\. Pause AI by Requesting Data Removal From Companies

We also recommend to pause AI by reducing the influence it has on your data. This can be done by requesting personal data deletion from businesses that retain it.

Privacy laws such as GDPR in Europe allow you to send data deletion requests from companies.
