# Introduction

On September 10th, [Apple](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/apple/ "View all posts in Apple") held its 2025 Fall Event, unveiling the iPhone 17 lineup alongside a brand-new product category—iPhone Air. Among the announcements, one product that stood out was [Apple](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/apple/ "View all posts in Apple")’s new [40W](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/40w/ "View all posts in 40W") Dynamic Power Adapter, which gained attention for its compact design and ability to dynamically deliver up to 60W of total output power.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440024.png)

The charger is now available on [Apple](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/apple/ "View all posts in Apple")’s official website, priced at $39 USD. According to [Apple](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/apple/ "View all posts in Apple"), when used with an iPhone 17 series device, the adapter can charge the phone to 50% in approximately 20 minutes—offering significantly faster charging performance compared to the previous generation. Despite the substantial power upgrade, the adapter maintains a highly compact form factor—roughly the same size as [Apple](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/apple/ "View all posts in Apple")’s standard 20W charger. It also features foldable prongs for improved portability and travel convenience. In the following teardown, we take a closer look at the internal design and power distribution architecture of Apple’s latest fast charger.

# Teardown

Next, let's take it apart to see its internal components and structure.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435847.jpg)

Remove the bottom casing, and it is revealed that the enclosure uses a combination of snap-fit clips and ultrasonic welding for sealing.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435830.jpg)

The input side features a contact-based power delivery design. The inner side of the casing is lined with a graphite thermal pad and has been treated for insulation.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440065.jpg)

Remove the PCBA module.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440037.jpg)

The inner side of the other half of the casing is lined with the graphite thermal insulation pad and the protective rubber cushion.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435710.jpg)

High-temperature resistant insulating tape is also applied along the sides.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435934.jpg)

The length of the PCBA module is about 42.12 mm (1.66 inches).

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435952.jpg)

The width is about 39.66 mm (1.56 inches).

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435775.jpg)

The thickness is about 23.27 mm (0.92 inches). 

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440148.jpg)

One side of the PCBA module and the top of the output end are covered with protective foam pads.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703441869.jpeg)

A protective pad is also placed between the two high-voltage electrolytic filter capacitors on the other side.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435797.jpg)

The top-mounted high-voltage electrolytic filter capacitor and the transformer are secured with adhesive for added stability and insulation.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435831.jpg)

The solid capacitors for output filtering are also secured with adhesive, and an insulating sheet is attached to the left side of the plastic casing.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435867.jpg)

Remove the foam pads and black adhesive. The transformer is wrapped in copper foil, and the side near the secondary winding is reinforced and insulated with a plastic casing.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435844.jpg)

The PCBA module's input side features metal contact plates for power delivery along with a supporting plastic frame.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435991.jpg)

This side contains common mode chokes, a capacitor supplying power to the master control chip, and Y capacitors.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440298.jpg)

On the opposite side, two high-voltage electrolytic capacitors are mounted horizontally.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435747.jpg)

On both sides of the output, there are Y capacitors and solid capacitors, respectively.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440048.jpg)

Remove the capacitors, transformer, and other components. A plastic bracket is installed at the bottom to provide support and isolate the PCB.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435898.jpg)

The front side of the PCB features an SMD fuse and the master control chip.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435925.jpg)

The back side of the PCB contains the bridge rectifier, differential mode choke, synchronous rectifier, protocol chip, and output VBUS MOSFET.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435825.jpg)

Disassemble the capacitors, transformer, and other components. Let’s take a closer look at the specifications and functions of these individual components.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440170.jpg)

The SMD fuse is from WalterFuse with a rated current of 3.15A.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435958.jpg)

The two common mode chokes are arranged in a two-stage design to more effectively filter out EMI interference.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435734-1.jpg)

The safety X2 capacitor is wrapped with copper foil for shielding.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435811.jpg)

It has a specification of 0.1μF.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435844-1.jpg)

It is connected via soldered copper strips.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440029.jpg)

The input-side bridge rectifier is from DIODES, model RTT10KL, and uses a TTL package.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435816.jpg)

The high-voltage filter capacitors are from NCC, each rated at 400V 47μF, connected in parallel.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440110.jpg)

The differential mode choke has a specification of 13μH.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440136.jpg)

The master control chip is from PI, marked with ZN1612F, a custom model for Apple. It integrates the primary controller, high-voltage MOSFET, synchronous rectifier controller, and feedback functions, offering high integration and reduced external components. This simplifies fast charging power circuit design. The chip uses an InSOP-T28D package.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435844.jpg)

The capacitor supplying power to the master control chip is from Lelon.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435717.jpg)

It is rated at 50V, 22μF.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435817.jpg)

The transformer is manufactured by Sumida.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435956.jpg)

Close-up of three black Y capacitors.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440251.jpg)

The synchronous rectifier is from RECTRON, model RM85N100DF. It is an NMOS transistor with a voltage rating of 100V and an on-resistance of 4.6mΩ, packaged in a DFN 5x6-8L form factor.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435882.jpg)

The three solid capacitors for output filtering are from Nichicon, each rated at 25V 270μF.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440052.jpg)

The protocol chip is from Infineon, model CYPDC1185B2-32E, housed in a 32-pin QFN package.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440165.jpg)

The TI TLV9001 is a single-channel, 1MHz, rail-to-rail input and output operational amplifier with a voltage range of 1.8–5.5V, packaged in a SOT-23 (5) form factor.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435917.jpg)

The VBUS MOSFET is also from RECTRON, model RM80N30DN. It is an NMOS transistor rated for 30V with an on-resistance of 3.8mΩ, packaged in a DFN 3x3 form factor.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703435971.jpg)

The thermistor monitors the internal temperature and dynamically adjusts the power level to reduce output power when the temperature rises.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440392.jpg)

Close-up of the sampling resistor used to detect the output current.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440127.jpg)

The pins of the USB-C socket are extended and soldered onto the PCBA.

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440061.jpg)

Well, those are all components of the Apple [40W](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/40w/ "View all posts in 40W") Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max.

# Summary of ChargerLAB

![Teardown of Apple 40W Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max (A3365)-Chargerlab](https://www.chargerlab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025091703440190.png)

Here is the component list of the Apple [40W](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/40w/ "View all posts in 40W") Dynamic Power Adapter with 60W Max for your convenience.

It features a design similar to the 35W dual USB-C charger and comes with foldable prongs. The unique dynamic power delivery can be understood as "peak 60W, stable [40W](https://www.chargerlab.com/tag/40w/ "View all posts in 40W")," intelligently adjusting output power based on the device’s needs. This ensures it meets the charging requirements of the iPhone while also accommodating high-power charging scenarios for the MacBook. Given the increased charging power of the iPhone 17 series, this new adapter is definitely worth considering for new device users.

After taking it apart, we found that it uses the highly integrated PI power chip ZN1612F, paired with the RECTRON RM85N100DF synchronous rectifier. The output is controlled by an Infineon CYPDC1185B2-32E protocol chip, with filtering handled by capacitors from NCC and Nichicon.

The internal thermistor monitors the internal temperature to enable dynamic power switching. The charger’s casing remains robust as usual, with graphite thermal pads and foam cushions applied on the inside to assist with heat dissipation and protection. The PCBA module is further reinforced and insulated with foam pads and a plastic bracket, demonstrating reliable workmanship.
