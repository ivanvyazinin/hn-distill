The **Cascata delle Marmore** (Italian: [\[kaˈskaːta delle ˈmarmore\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian "Help:IPA/Italian")) or **Marmore Falls** is a tiered, [man-made waterfall](/wiki/Artificial_waterfall "Artificial waterfall") in [Italy](/wiki/Italy "Italy"), created by the [Romans](/wiki/Ancient_Rome "Ancient Rome") in 271 BC. At 165m (541 feet) tall, it is the [largest man-made waterfall in the world](/wiki/List_of_waterfalls_by_height "List of waterfalls by height").[\[1\]](#cite_note-1) It is located 7.7 km from [Terni](/wiki/Terni "Terni"), in the region of [Umbria](/wiki/Umbria "Umbria").[\[2\]](#cite_note-2)

## History

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Cascata_delle_Marmore&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

In ancient times, the [Velino River](/wiki/Velino "Velino") fed a [wetland](/wiki/Wetland "Wetland") in the [Rieti Valley](/wiki/Rieti_Valley "Rieti Valley"). In 271 BC, in order to reclaim the land (and to possibly remove the supposed threat of [malaria](/wiki/Malaria "Malaria") to the nearby city of [Rieti](/wiki/Rieti "Rieti")\[_[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]), the [Roman consul](/wiki/Roman_consul "Roman consul") [Manius Curius Dentatus](/wiki/Curius_Dentatus "Curius Dentatus") ordered the construction of a [canal](/wiki/Canal "Canal") (the _Curiano Trench_) to divert the stagnant waters over the cliff near [Marmore](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marmore_\(Terni\) "it:Marmore (Terni)"), thus directing it into the [Nera River](/wiki/Nera_\(Tiber\) "Nera (Tiber)") below.[\[3\]](#cite_note-3)

Lack of maintenance in the canal resulted in a decrease of flow that eventually allowed the wetland to reappear. In 1422, [Pope Gregory XII](/wiki/Pope_Gregory_XII "Pope Gregory XII") ordered the construction of a new canal to restore the original flow (the _Gregorian Trench_ or _Rieti Trench_). In 1545, [Pope Paul III](/wiki/Pope_Paul_III "Pope Paul III") ordered that a new canal be built (the _Pauline Trench_). The plan was to expand the _Curiano Trench_ and to build a regulating valve to control the flow. Upon its completion in 1598, [Pope Clement VIII](/wiki/Pope_Clement_VIII "Pope Clement VIII") inaugurated the new work and named it after himself: the _Clementine Trench_. Over the following two centuries, the canal and the Nera often created flooding issues for the valley below. In 1787, [Pope Pius VI](/wiki/Pope_Pius_VI "Pope Pius VI") ordered architect [Andrea Vici](/wiki/Andrea_Vici "Andrea Vici") to modify the leaps below the falls, giving the falls their present look and finally resolving the majority of the problems.

[Lord Byron](/wiki/Lord_Byron "Lord Byron") visited the waterfall and referenced it in the narrative poem _[Childe Harold's Pilgrimage](/wiki/Childe_Harold%27s_Pilgrimage "Childe Harold's Pilgrimage")_, published between 1812 and 1818.[\[4\]](#cite_note-4)

In 1896, the newly formed steel mills in Terni began using the water flow in the _Curiano Trench_ to power their operation. In the following years, engineers began using the water flow to generate electricity\[_[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\].

## Current status

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Cascata_delle_Marmore&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Current status")\]

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Incredible_rainbow_over_the_waterfall.jpg/250px-Incredible_rainbow_over_the_waterfall.jpg)](/wiki/File:Incredible_rainbow_over_the_waterfall.jpg)

A [rainbow](/wiki/Rainbow "Rainbow") seen in the falls

Typically, the water in the canals above the falls is diverted to the Galleto hydroelectric power plant, reducing the flow over the falls. Established in 1929, the Galleto plant has a capacity of roughly 530 MW. Piediluco Lake, situated above the falls, acts as a reservoir for this power plant. To manage the power plant's operation and cater to tourists, the falls operate on a set schedule\[_[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\].

The falls usually flow from 12:00 to 13:00 and again from 16:00 to 17:00 daily, with extended hours on holidays. There is an entrance fee to access the falls and its nearby areas. Visitors can follow a path leading to the falls' summit. En route, a tunnel provides access to an observatory adjacent to the falls. Another observatory near the peak offers a panoramic view of both the falls and the Nera Valley below\[_[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\].
