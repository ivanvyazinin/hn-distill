# Forever Object: The Staple-less Oceanus Brass Stapler

By [Rain Noe](https://codex.core77.com/users/Rain-Noe) - October 23

in [Object Culture](https://www.core77.com/Object-Culture?utm_source=from_tags)

More evidence that people are seeking forever objects: The success of this [Oceanus Brass Stapler](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/oceanusbrass/stapler/description), by the company that brought you [that 20-pound stainless steel drinking mug.](https://www.core77.com/posts/135048/Extreme-Forever-Object-A-20-Pound-Stainless-Steel-Drinking-Mug)

![](https://s3files.core77.com/blog/images/1770043_81_139027_EiFyOeqdH.jpeg)

![](https://s3files.core77.com/blog/images/1770044_81_139027_FWzl9o006.jpeg)

Machined out of an inch-thick block of brass, the staple-less stapler uses a punch that perforates the paper and folds a tab over to lock the pages together.

![](https://s3files.core77.com/blog/images/1770047_81_139027_DPwSQn2ko.png)

![](https://s3files.core77.com/blog/images/1770046_81_139027_J5YSWgaaE.png)

"Ergonomically shaped for one-handed use, the Oceanus Brass Stapler is balanced and deeply satisfying to use," the company writes.

The diminutive stapler is just 2 ¾" square by an inch thick, but being brass, weighs 13 oz. Not as heavy as that mug, but will certainly last you just as long.

_Enter a caption (optional)_

The $90 stapler has been a rousing success on Kickstarter; at press time it had garnered $160,000 with 26 days left to pledge.
