# Tech megacaps lose $770 billion in value as Nasdaq suffers steepest drop since April

Published Fri, Oct 10 20255:11 PM EDTUpdated Sat, Oct 11 20251:24 AM EDT

![thumbnail](https://image.cnbcfm.com/api/v1/image/101872355-Ari-Levy-Silo-BG.jpg?v=1614082071&w=60&h=60&ffmt=webp)

[Ari Levy](https://www.cnbc.com/ari-levy/)[@levynews](https://twitter.com/@levynews)

![thumbnail](https://image.cnbcfm.com/api/v1/image/105119178-Jordan_Novet_IMG_4402a.jpg?v=1523299470&w=60&h=60&ffmt=webp)

[Jordan Novet](https://www.cnbc.com/jordan-novet/)[@in/jordannovet/](https://linkedin.com/in/jordannovet/)

[WATCH LIVE](/live-tv/)

Key Points

*   Nvidia, Amazon and Tesla all fell by about 5% on Friday, pushing the Nasdaq down 3.6%, its worst day since April.
*   The slide came after President Trump threatened to slap a “massive increase of Tariffs” on Chinese products imported into the U.S.
*   Trump later declared that the U.S. would impose a 100% tariff on China and, on Nov. 1, would apply export controls "on any and all critical software," pushing the technology stocks lower after hours.

Shares of [Amazon](/quotes/AMZN/), [Nvidia](/quotes/NVDA/) and [Tesla](/quotes/TSLA/) each dropped around 5% on Friday, as tech's megacaps lost $770 billion in market cap, following President [Donald Trump's threats](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/10/trump-china-tariffs-rare-earths.html) for increased tariffs on Chinese goods.

With tech's trillion-dollar companies occupying an increasingly large slice of the U.S. market, their declines sent the Nasdaq down 3.6% and the S&P 500 down 2.7%. For both indexes, it was the worst day since April, when Trump said he would slap ["reciprocal" duties](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/03/31/trump-tariffs-reciprocal-april-2.html) on U.S. trading partners.

After market close on Friday, Trump declared in a social media post that the U.S. would impose a 100% [tariff](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/115351840469973590) on [China](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/10/trump-trade-tariffs-china-software.html) and, on Nov. 1, it would apply export controls "on any and all critical software."

Amazon, Nvidia and Tesla all slipped about 2% in extended trading following the post.

The president's latest threats are disrupting, at least briefly, what had been a sustained rally in tech, built on hundreds of billions of dollars in planned spending on [artificial intelligence](https://www.cnbc.com/ai-effect/) infrastructure.

In late September, Nvidia, which makes graphics processing units for training AI models, became [the first company](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/09/30/nvidias-market-cap-tops-4point5-trillion-on-ai-infrastructure-deals.html) to reach a market cap of $4.5 trillion. Nvidia alone saw its market capitalization decline by nearly $229 billion on Friday.

OpenAI counts on Nvidia's GPUs from a series of cloud suppliers, including [Microsoft](/quotes/MSFT/). OpenAI is only seeing rising demand.

In September it introduced the [Sora 2](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/02/openai-invite-code-sora-2-censorship.html) video creation app, and this week the company said the ChatGPT assistant now boasts [over 800 million](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/06/open-ai-devday-live-updates-altman-jony-ive.html) weekly users. Microsoft, which spends heavily on infrastructure to operate its cloud data centers, saw its market cap drop by $85 billion on Friday.

The sell-off wiped out [Amazon's](/quotes/AMZN/) gains for the year. That stock is now down 2% so far in 2025. Amazon competes with Microsoft to rent out GPUs from its cloud data centers. The online retailer lost $121 billion in value during the day.

"There continues to be a lot of noise about the impact that tariffs will have on retail prices and consumption," Amazon CEO Andy Jassy [told analysts](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/07/31/amazon-amzn-q2-earnings-report-2025.html) in July. "Much of it thus far has been wrong and misreported. As we said before, it's impossible to know what will happen."

Tesla, which introduced [lower-priced vehicles](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/07/tesla-stock-roadster-budget-model-y.html) on Tuesday, saw its market capitalization sink by $71 billion.

The automaker reports third-quarter results on Oct. 22, with Microsoft earnings scheduled for the following week. Nvidia reports in November.

Google parent [Alphabet](/quotes/GOOGL/) and Facebook owner [Meta](/quotes/META/) fell 2% and almost 4%, respectively.
