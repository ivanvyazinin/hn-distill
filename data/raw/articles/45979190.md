# Larry Summers resigns from OpenAI board after release of emails with Epstein

Published Wed, Nov 19 20257:10 AM ESTUpdated Wed, Nov 19 20259:19 AM EST

![thumbnail](https://image.cnbcfm.com/api/v1/image/107134095-1665676148728-2022_Headshots-0474.jpg?v=1665676160&w=60&h=60&ffmt=webp)

[Ashley Capoot](https://www.cnbc.com/ashley-capoot/)[@/in/ashley-capoot/](https://linkedin.com//in/ashley-capoot/)

[WATCH LIVE](/live-tv/)

Key Points

*   Former Treasury Secretary Larry Summers said Wednesday that he will resign from the board of OpenAI.
*   Details about Summers' communications with sex offender Jeffrey Epstein were made public last week.
*   Summers had previously announced that he would be stepping back from all public commitments, but it was not clear at the time if that included his role at OpenAI.

Larry Summers, president emeritus and professor at Harvard University, at the World Economic Forum (WEF) in Davos, Switzerland, on Tuesday, Jan. 21, 2025. 

Stefan Wermuth | Bloomberg | Getty Images

Former [Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/) Secretary [Larry Summers](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/14/trump-doj-jeffrey-epstein-clinton-jpmorgan-summers.html) said Wednesday that he will resign from the board of OpenAI after the release of [emails](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/13/goldman-sachs-jeffrey-epstein-emails-ruemmler.html) between him and the notorious sex offender [Jeffrey Epstein](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/12/trump-jeffrey-epstein-ghislaine-maxwell-emails-house-democrats.html).

Summers had announced Monday that he would be [stepping back](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/17/larry-summers-jeffrey-epstein-harvard.html) from all public commitments, but it was not immediately clear whether that included his position at the [artificial intelligence](https://www.cnbc.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/) startup.

"I am grateful for the opportunity to have served, excited about the potential of the company, and look forward to following their progress," Summers said in a statement to CNBC. 

OpenAI's board told CNBC it respects Summers' decision to resign.

"We appreciate his many contributions and the perspective he brought to the Board," the OpenAI board of directors said in a statement.

Details of Summers' correspondence with Epstein were made public last week after the House Oversight and Government Reform Committee released more than 20,000 documents it obtained pursuant to a subpoena from Epstein's estate. Summers has faced intense scrutiny following the release of those files.

Summers joined OpenAI's board in 2023 during a [turbulent period](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/11/22/sam-altmans-back-heres-whos-on-the-new-openai-board-and-whos-out.html) for the startup. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman was briefly ousted from the company, though he returned to the chief executive role days later. 

In the wake of "The Blip," as some OpenAI employees call it, Summers was appointed to the board alongside Bret Taylor, former co-CEO of [Salesforce](/quotes/CRM/), and Quora CEO Adam D'Angelo, who was the only member of OpenAI's previous board who still held a seat.

[Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/11/19/epstein-larry-summers-openai) was first to report about Summers' resignation from the board.

President [Donald Trump](https://www.cnbc.com/donald-trump/) on Friday asked the Department of Justice to [investigate the relationship](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/14/trump-doj-jeffrey-epstein-clinton-jpmorgan-summers.html) between Epstein and Summers, as well as Epstein's ties to former President [Bill Clinton](https://www.cnbc.com/bill-clinton/), J[PMorgan Chase](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/JPM/) and billionaire tech investor [Reid Hoffman](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/20/openai-investor-reid-hoffman-calls-anthropic-one-of-the-good-guys.html). Trump has been facing renewed pressure over his own past friendship with Epstein.

Summers is a former president of [Harvard University](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/05/27/harvard-trump-administration-cut-federal-contracts.html), and Democratic Sen. Elizabeth Warren of Massachusetts told CNN on Monday that the university should [sever ties](https://www.cnn.com/2025/11/17/politics/elizabeth-warren-larry-summers-harvard-epstein) with him. He announced his intention to step back from his public commitments later that day, but said he will continue to fulfill his teaching obligations at Harvard.

"I am deeply ashamed of my actions and recognize the pain they have caused. I take full responsibility for my misguided decision to continue communicating with Mr. Epstein," Summers said in a statement to CNBC on Monday.

Congress on Tuesday agreed to pass a [bipartisan bill](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/18/epstein-files-trump-house-vote.html) ordering the [Department of Justice](https://www.justice.gov/) to release all of its files on Epstein, clearing the path for Trump to sign it into law.
