**Tenacity** is an easy-to-use, cross-platform multi-track audio editor/recorder for Windows, Linux, and other operating systems and is developed by a group of volunteers as open-source software.

## Features

 ![](images/feature-icons/recording.png)

**Recording**  
from audio devices (real or virtual)

 ![](images/feature-icons/export-import.png)

**Export & Import**  
a wide range of audio formats (extendible with FFmpeg)

 ![](images/feature-icons/high-quality.png)

**High Quality**  
including up to 32-bit float audio support

 ![](images/feature-icons/plugins.png)

**Plug-Ins**  
providing support for VST, LV2, and AU plugins

 ![](images/feature-icons/scripting.png)

**Scripting**  
in the built-in scripting language Nyquist, or in Python, Perl and other languages with named pipes

 ![](images/feature-icons/editing.png)

**Editing**  
arbitrary sampling and multi-track timeline

 ![](images/feature-icons/accessibility.png)

**Accessibility**  
including editing via keyboard, screen reader support and narration support

 ![](images/feature-icons/tools.svg)

**Tools**  
useful in the analysis of signals, including audio

## Getting started

Pre-packaged builds are available through the [releases page](https://codeberg.org/tenacityteam/tenacity/releases) on the repository. Tenacity might also be packaged for your software distribution.

Alternatively, you can build Tenacity [from source](https://codeberg.org/tenacityteam/tenacity/src/main/BUILDING.md).

## Development

The upstream development repository is found on Codeberg. We maintain a GitHub mirror for accessibility and CI purposes, but pull requests are ignored.  
  
To contribute to Tenacity, please read the [contributing guidelines](https://codeberg.org/tenacityteam/tenacity/src/branch/main/CONTRIBUTING.md).
