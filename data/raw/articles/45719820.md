# Canada Set to Side With China On EVs

## The Ottawa government is considering dropping 100 percent tariffs on imports of Chinese EVs, in a potentially major break with the U.S. approach.

By [Eliot Chen](https://www.thewirechina.com/team_member/eliot-chen/) — October 26, 2025

[Politics](https://www.thewirechina.com/topic/politics/ "View all posts in Politics")[Trade](https://www.thewirechina.com/topic/trade/ "View all posts in Trade")

![](https://www.thewirechina.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Mark-Carney-Li-Qiang-Meeting-September-2025-scaled.jpg)

Canada's Prime Minister Mark Carney meets with Chinese Premier Li Qiang on the sidelines of the United Nations General Assembly, in New York, September 23, 2025. _Credit: Sean Kilpatrick/The Canadian Press via [AP Images](https://newsroom.ap.org/detail/UNGeneralAssembly/f4cddb5fee5b48a6a0f25fffa80a9909/photo)_

China has become an automaking powerhouse, but thanks to steep tariffs on its electric vehicles, most North American consumers have yet to see its cars on their roads. That may be about to change.  Canada’s government is considering removing the country’s 100 percent tariff on Chinese EVs. The potential pivot comes as President Donald Trump’s trade war is wreaking havoc on the deeply integrated U.S.-Canada auto supply chain, and as Canada looks for ways to negotiate down trade barr
