![Mark Zuckerberg and President Donald Trump](https://i.pcmag.com/imagery/articles/07DxDO8QdZ9KK2TjmCmLtgv-1.fit_lim.v1757103289.jpg) (Credit: Alex Wong / Staff / Getty Images News via Getty Images)

It's not unusual for tech company CEOs to make the journey to Washington, DC, and announce [billion-dollar investments](https://www.pcmag.com/news/openai-softbank-build-ai-data-centers-500-billion-stargate-trump) to curry favor with politicians in power. Apple CEO Tim Cook was in the Oval Office last month, a piece of Apple-shaped glass and a 24-karat gold base in hand, to [pledge another $100 billion](https://www.pcmag.com/news/trump-get-ready-for-100-tariffs-on-foreign-chips) in US investment over the next four years, for a total of $600 billion.

Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg perhaps had that number on his mind this week when he joined his fellow Silicon Valley heavy hitters at the White House for a [dinner](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/09/president-trump-tech-leaders-unite-american-ai-dominance/ "(Opens in a new tab)") with President Trump. Zuckerberg was seated next to the president, who at one point leaned over and asked him, "How much are you spending, would you say, over the next few years?"

A flustered Zuckerberg responded, "Oh gosh, um, I mean, I think it's probably going to be something like, at least $600 billion through '28 in the US, yeah."

"That's a lot, that's a lot," Trump said.

It is indeed. Once the discussion concluded, Zuckerberg leaned over to Trump to privately admit the president had caught him off guard. "I'm sorry I wasn't ready...I wasn't sure what number you wanted to go with," Zuckerberg said in a revealing moment caught on a hot mic.

> [This Tweet is currently unavailable. It might be loading or has been removed.](https://twitter.com/AutismCapital/status/1964029070142066999 "(Opens in a new tab)")

Meta would have to dramatically ramp up its AI spending to hit $600 billion in the next three years. During a July earnings call, Meta estimated its full-year 2025 total expenses to be between $114 billion and $118 billion. At the time, CFO Susan Li [said](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4806454-meta-platforms-inc-meta-q2-2025-earnings-call-transcript "(Opens in a new tab)") Meta had not "kicked off our budgeting process for 2026." But the biggest cost drivers for next year will be [infrastructure costs](https://www.pcmag.com/news/not-one-but-several-zuckerberg-eyes-more-mega-data-centers-for-ai) and [employee compensation](https://www.pcmag.com/news/mark-zuckerberg-to-openai-researchers-join-me-for-100-million), she said. Li estimated a growth rate of 20% to 24% year-over-year, so $600 billion by 2028 would be quite ambitious.

Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella [said](https://x.com/mommaleo/status/1963769915791348134 "(Opens in a new tab)") the company is close to $80 billion per year in the US, a number that's been [consistent](https://blogs.microsoft.com/on-the-issues/2025/01/03/the-golden-opportunity-for-american-ai/ "(Opens in a new tab)") since at least January 2025.

OpenAI Sam Altman was also there, but his [online sparring partner](https://www.pcmag.com/news/musks-xai-sues-apple-openai-for-depriving-grok-of-ai-prompt-volume-monopolists), Elon Musk, did not get an invite, The Hill [reports](https://thehill.com/homenews/administration/5485218-trump-host-tech-ceos-rose-garden/ "(Opens in a new tab)"). Amazon CEO Andy Jassy was also not in attendance.
