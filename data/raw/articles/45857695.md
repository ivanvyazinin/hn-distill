# Bell bottom-era tape unearthed, could contain lost piece of Unix history

[**25** ![comment bubble on white](/design_picker/f5daacc84b9722c1e31ba85f836c37e4ad993fc4/graphics/icons/bubble_comment_white.png)](https://forums.theregister.com/forum/all/2025/11/07/unix_fourth_edition_tape_rediscovered/ "View comments on this article") 

## It might have the first-ever version of UNIX written in C

![icon](/design_picker/d518b499f8a6e2c65d4d8c49aca8299d54b03012/graphics/icon/vulture_red.svg) [Liam Proven](/Author/Liam-Proven "Read more by this author")

Fri 7 Nov 2025 // 18:46 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

[](https://www.reddit.com/submit?url=https://www.theregister.com/2025/11/07/unix_fourth_edition_tape_rediscovered/%3futm_medium%3dshare%26utm_content%3darticle%26utm_source%3dreddit&title=Bell%20bottom-era%20tape%20unearthed%2c%20could%20contain%20lost%20piece%20of%20Unix%20history)[](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=Bell%20bottom-era%20tape%20unearthed%2c%20could%20contain%20lost%20piece%20of%20Unix%20history&url=https://www.theregister.com/2025/11/07/unix_fourth_edition_tape_rediscovered/%3futm_medium%3dshare%26utm_content%3darticle%26utm_source%3dtwitter&via=theregister)[](https://www.facebook.com/dialog/feed?app_id=1404095453459035&display=popup&link=https://www.theregister.com/2025/11/07/unix_fourth_edition_tape_rediscovered/%3futm_medium%3dshare%26utm_content%3darticle%26utm_source%3dfacebook)  
[](https://www.linkedin.com/shareArticle?mini=true&url=https://www.theregister.com/2025/11/07/unix_fourth_edition_tape_rediscovered/%3futm_medium%3dshare%26utm_content%3darticle%26utm_source%3dlinkedin&title=Bell%20bottom-era%20tape%20unearthed%2c%20could%20contain%20lost%20piece%20of%20Unix%20history&summary=It%20might%20have%20the%20first-ever%20version%20of%20UNIX%20written%20in%20C)[](https://api.whatsapp.com/send?text=https://www.theregister.com/2025/11/07/unix_fourth_edition_tape_rediscovered/%3futm_medium%3dshare%26utm_content%3darticle%26utm_source%3dwhatsapp)

A tape-based piece of unique Unix history may have been lying quietly in storage at the University of Utah for 50+ years. The question is whether researchers will be able to take this piece of middle-aged media and rewind it back to the 1970s to get the data off.

The news was [posted to Mastodon](https://discuss.systems/@ricci/115504720054699983) by [Professor Robert Ricci](https://ricci.io/) of the University of Utah's [Kahlert School of Computing](https://www.cs.utah.edu/).

> While cleaning a storage room, our staff found this tape containing #UNIX v4 from Bell Labs, circa 1973
> 
> Apparently no other complete copies are known to exist: [https://gunkies.org/wiki/UNIX\_Fourth\_Edition](https://gunkies.org/wiki/UNIX_Fourth_Edition)
> 
> We have arranged to deliver it to the Computer History Museum

The nine-track tape reel bears a handwritten label reading:

**UNIX Original From Bell Labs V4 (See Manual for format)**

Ricci says that the handwriting on the label is that of his former advisor [Jay Lepreau](http://ccr.sigcomm.org/online/files/p45-v39n1j-andersen.pdf) \[PDF\], who [died of multiple myeloma in 2008](https://web.archive.org/web/20151031110707/http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/saltlaketribune/obituary.aspx?page=lifestory&pid=117597321).

[![](https://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ad?co=1&iu=/6978/reg_software/oses&sz=300x50%7C300x100%7C300x250%7C300x251%7C300x252%7C300x600%7C300x601&tile=2&c=2aQ97iafHCj3Nv613Kqe6-QAAAYQ&t=ct%3Dns%26unitnum%3D2%26raptor%3Dcondor%26pos%3Dtop%26test%3D0)](https://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/jump?co=1&iu=/6978/reg_software/oses&sz=300x50%7C300x100%7C300x250%7C300x251%7C300x252%7C300x600%7C300x601&tile=2&c=2aQ97iafHCj3Nv613Kqe6-QAAAYQ&t=ct%3Dns%26unitnum%3D2%26raptor%3Dcondor%26pos%3Dtop%26test%3D0)

If it's what it says on the label, this is a notable discovery because [little of UNIX V4](https://minnie.tuhs.org/cgi-bin/utree.pl?file=V4) remains. That's unfortunate as this specific version is especially interesting: it's the first version of UNIX in which the kernel and some of the core utilities were rewritten in the new C programming language. Until now, the only surviving parts known were the [source code](https://minnie.tuhs.org/cgi-bin/utree.pl?file=V4/nsys) to a slightly older version of the kernel and a few [`man` pages](https://minnie.tuhs.org/cgi-bin/utree.pl?file=V4/man) – plus the [Programmer's Manual](https://dspinellis.github.io/unix-v4man/v4man.pdf) \[PDF\], from November 1973.

[![](https://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ad?co=1&iu=/6978/reg_software/oses&sz=300x50%7C300x100%7C300x250%7C300x251%7C300x252%7C300x600%7C300x601&tile=3&c=33aQ97iafHCj3Nv613Kqe6-QAAAYQ&t=ct%3Dns%26unitnum%3D3%26raptor%3Deagle%26pos%3Dmid%26test%3D0)](https://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/jump?co=1&iu=/6978/reg_software/oses&sz=300x50%7C300x100%7C300x250%7C300x251%7C300x252%7C300x600%7C300x601&tile=3&c=33aQ97iafHCj3Nv613Kqe6-QAAAYQ&t=ct%3Dns%26unitnum%3D3%26raptor%3Deagle%26pos%3Dmid%26test%3D0)

More was to follow – some hours later, [he continued](https://discuss.systems/@ricci/115509057505895666):

> We have some more information on this! One of [@regehr](https://mastodon.social/@regehr)'s [grad students](https://cv.thalia.dev/) did some excellent sleuthing and figured out that this was received by Martin Newell: [https://archive.org/details/unix\_news\_july-30-1975/page/n9/mode/2up](https://archive.org/details/unix_news_july-30-1975/page/n9/mode/2up)
> 
> If that name sounds familiar to you, it's probably because his teapot is ubiquitous in computer graphics: [https://graphics.cs.utah.edu/teapot/](https://graphics.cs.utah.edu/teapot/)

So, this is the original copy of UNIX Fourth Edition received from AT&T by the inventor of the Utah Teapot – as seen in the [original Windows NT OpenGL screensaver](https://www.theregister.com/2024/06/13/windows_3d_pipes_screensaver/).

Ricci [said](https://discuss.systems/@ricci/115505025036183624):

> The staff member who found it is planning to drive it to the CHM, rather than ship it.

That being the [Computer History Museum](https://computerhistory.org/) in Mountain View, California – which Google Maps tells us is a mere 771 miles away, a modest 12 hours' drive. The software librarian at the CHM is the redoubtable [Al Kossow of Bitsavers](https://terminals-wiki.org/wiki/index.php/BitSavers), who [commented](https://oldbytes.space/@bitsavers/115505135441862982) in the thread that he is on the case. On the TUHS mailing list, he [explained](https://www.tuhs.org/pipermail/tuhs/2025-November/032765.html) how he plans to do it:

> taping off the head read amplifier, using a multi-channel high speed analog to digital converter which dumps into 100-ish gigabytes of RAM, then an analysis program Len Shustek wrote: [https://github.com/LenShustek/readtape](https://github.com/LenShustek/readtape)
> 
> It is a '70s 1200ft 3M tape, likely 9 track, which has a pretty good chance of being recoverable.

He also [noted](https://www.tuhs.org/pipermail/tuhs/2025-November/032764.html):

> This is rare enough that I'm pushing the recovery of it up near the top of my project queue.

The _Reg_ FOSS desk has occasionally corresponded with Kossow, and we feel sure that this precious find could be in no better hands. ®
