# First LinHT tests

Written by

[Wojciech Kaczmarski](https://m17project.org/author/wkaczmarski/)

in

[Hardware](https://m17project.org/category/hardware/), [News](https://m17project.org/category/news/)

The future is here: LinHT booted up successfully for the first time. We believe that _Software Defined Transceivers_ will be the next big trend in amateur radio.

> _LinHT is the most important hardware project in Amateur Radio today._  
> Bruce Perens, K6BP
> 
> [https://perens.com/2025/08/12/whats-wrong-with-ardc/](https://perens.com/2025/08/12/whats-wrong-with-ardc/)

The test device has no RF amplifier (although we will include [GRF5604 RF amplifier](https://www.guerrilla-rf.com/products/detail/sku/GRF5604) in the next revision, see [this](https://github.com/M17-Project/LinHT-rf-amp)). The output power of this test setup is around 5dBm. Frequency range: 420-450MHz (UHF).

While some doubted that such a design is even viable, we continued our work tirelessly. Big shout out to Vlastimil OK5VAS and Andreas OE3ANC. The project would not be possible without your help, guys.

![](https://m17project.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/linht_1-558x1024.jpg)

Quick, preliminary LinHT preview.

The device is open source hardware. The PCB design is available [here](https://github.com/M17-Project/LinHT-hw).

Total cost for a prototype run:  
– $490 for PCB+assembly at PCBWay (5 pcs.)  
– Retevis C62 (donor)  
– $469 for SoM, 5pcs.
