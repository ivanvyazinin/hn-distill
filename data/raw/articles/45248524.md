# Welcome to Xerox Nostalgia

![Xerox 914](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/storage/2020/03/Xerox_914_600pix-300x240.jpg)

Xerox is the company that brought the first plain paper copier to the world. The process for making copies on plain paper, was first called Electro-photography, but was later changed to Xerography.

This website aims to preserve the rich history of Xerox, highlighting both the company and the early machines it produced. We invite you to explore this showcase, which features the legacy of Xerox's early copiers and duplicators, along with the story of Xerox and its pioneering role in xerography.

Many thanks to Xerox Archivist Ray Brewer, for providing most of the images that is seen on this website.

## [**Xerox 50 series**](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/category/50-series/)

In 1988 Xerox launched their [50 series](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/category/50-series/), to celebrate Fifty years after Chester Carlson produced the first xerographic image in his Astoria, Queens, New York lab.  Xerox called the new copiers, grouped under the 50 series label, its most important new products since 1982, when it announced the first of its 10 series of copiers.

The new models had more features and cost more than models in the 10 series, which helped Xerox win back much of the market it had lost to Japanese rivals. The first models in the 50 series was [5018](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/xerox-5018/), [5028](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/xerox-5028/), [5046](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/xerox-5046/), [5052](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/xerox-5052/) and [5090](https://xeroxnostalgia.com/xerox-5090/).
