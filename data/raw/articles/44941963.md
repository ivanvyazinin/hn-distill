Who Invented Backpropagation?

* * *

Efficient backpropagation (BP) is central to the ongoing [Neural Network (NN) ReNNaissance and "Deep Learning."](http://www.idsia.ch/~juergen/deeplearning.html) Who invented it?

BP's modern version (also called the reverse mode of automatic differentiation) was first published in 1970 by Finnish master student **Seppo Linnainmaa** [\[BP1\]](#BP1) [\[R7\]](#R7). **In 2020, we celebrated BP's half-century anniversary!** A precursor of BP was published by Henry J. Kelley in 1960 [\[BPA\]](#BPA)—in 2020, we celebrated its 60-year anniversary.

In the 2020s, it was still easy to find misleading accounts of BP's history [\[HIN\]](#HIN)[\[T22\]](#T22)[\[DLP\]](#DLP)[\[NOB\]](#NOB). I had a look at the original papers from the 1960s and 70s, and talked to BP pioneers. Here is a summary based on my award-winning [2014 survey](deep-learning-overview.html) [\[DL1\]](#DL1) which includes most of the references mentioned below.

The minimisation of errors through gradient descent (Cauchy 1847 [\[GD'\]](#GD'), Hadamard, 1908 [\[GD''\]](#GD'')) in the parameter space of complex, nonlinear, differentiable, multi-stage, NN-related systems has been discussed at least since the early 1960s, e.g., Kelley (1960) [\[BPA\]](#BPA); Bryson (1961) [\[BPB\]](#BPB); Pontryagin et al. (1961); Dreyfus (1962) [\[BPC\]](#BPC); Wilkinson (1965); Tsypkin (1966) [\[GDa-b\]](#GDa); Amari (1967-68) [\[GD2,GD2a\]](#GD2); Bryson and Ho (1969); initially within the framework of Euler-LaGrange equations in the Calculus of Variations, e.g., Euler (1744).

Steepest descent in the weight space of such systems can be performed (Kelley, 1960 [\[BPA\]](#BPA); Bryson, 1961 [\[BPB\]](#BPB)) by iterating the chain rule ([Leibniz](leibniz-father-computer-science-375.html), 1676 [\[LEI07-10\]](#LEI07)[\[DLH\]](#DLH); L'Hopital, 1696) in Dynamic Programming style (DP, e.g., Bellman, 1957 [\[BEL53\]](#BEL53)). A simplified derivation (Dreyfus, 1962 [\[BPC\]](#BPC)) of this backpropagation method uses only the [Leibniz](leibniz-father-computer-science-375.html) chain rule [\[LEI07\]](#LEI07).

The systems of the 1960s were already efficient in the DP sense. However, they backpropagated derivative information through standard Jacobian matrix calculations from one "layer" to the previous one, without explicitly addressing either direct links across several layers or potential additional efficiency gains due to network sparsity.

Explicit, efficient error backpropagation (BP) in arbitrary, discrete, possibly sparsely connected, NN-like networks was first described in a 1970 master's thesis (Linnainmaa, 1970, 1976) [\[BP1\]](#BP1)[\[R7\]](#R7), albeit without reference to NNs. This kind of BP is also known as the _reverse mode of automatic differentiation_ (e.g., Griewank, 2012 [\[BP5\]](#BP5)), where the costs of forward activation spreading essentially equal the costs of backward derivative calculation. See early BP FORTRAN code (Linnainmaa, 1970) [\[BP1\]](#BP1) and closely related but slightly later work by Ostrovskii et al. (1971) [\[BP1a\]](#BP1a) (apparently the _first journal publication_ on backpropagation). As of 2020, all modern software packages for NNs (such as Google's Tensorflow) are based on Linnainmaa's method of 1970.

BP was soon explicitly used to minimize cost functions by adapting control parameters (weights) (Dreyfus, 1973). This was followed by some preliminary, NN-specific discussion (Werbos, 1974, section 5.5.1) and a computer program for automatically deriving and implementing BP in differentiable systems (Speelpenning, 1980). The first NN-specific application of efficient BP as above was apparently described by Werbos in 1982 [\[BP2\]](#BP2) (but not yet in his 1974 thesis, as is sometimes claimed).

However, already in 1967, Amari suggested to train deep multilayer perceptrons (MLPs) with many layers in non-incremental end-to-end fashion from scratch by stochastic gradient descent (SGD) [\[GD1\]](#GD1), a method proposed in 1951 [\[STO51-52\]](#STO51). Amari's implementation [\[GD2,GD2a\]](#GD2) (with his student Saito) learned _internal representations_ in a five layer MLP with two modifiable layers [\[DLH\]](#DLH)[\[NOB\]](#NOB), which was trained to classify non-linearily separable pattern classes. Back then compute was billions of times more expensive than today.

Compare the first deep learning MLPs called GMDH networks (Ivakhnenko and Lapa, since 1965) whose layers are incrementally grown and trained by regression analysis [\[DEEP1-2\]](#DEEP1)[\[R8\]](#R8)[\[DLH\]](#DLH)[\[NOB\]](#NOB). These were actually the first deep NNs that learned to create hierarchical, distributed, _internal representations_ of incoming data.

Additional work on backpropagation was published later (e.g., Parker, 1985; LeCun, 1985). By 1985, compute was about 1,000 times cheaper than in 1970 [\[BP1\]](#BP1), and the first desktop computers became accessible in wealthier academic labs. An experimental analysis of the known method [\[BP1-2\]](#BP1) by Rumelhart et al. then demonstrated that backpropagation can yield useful internal representations in hidden layers of NNs [\[RUM\]](#RUM). At least for supervised learning, this tends to be more efficient than Amari's above-mentioned deep learning through the more general SGD method (1967), which learned useful internal representations in NNs about 2 decades earlier [\[GD1-2a\]](#GD1).

Some ask: _"Isn't backpropagation just the chain rule of [Leibniz](leibniz-father-computer-science-375.html) (1676) [\[LEI07-10\]](#LEI07) & L'Hopital (1696)?"_ No, it is the efficient way of applying the chain rule to big networks with differentiable nodes—see [Sec. XII](scientific-integrity-turing-award-deep-learning.html#XII) of [\[T22\]](#T22)[\[DLH\]](#DLH)). (There are also many inefficient ways of doing this.) It was not published until 1970 [\[BP1\]](#BP1).

It took 4 decades until the backpropagation method of 1970 [\[BP1-2\]](#BP1) got widely accepted as a training method for deep NNs. Before 2010, many thought that the training of NNs with many layers requires [unsupervised pre-training](deep-learning-miraculous-year-1990-1991.html#Sec.%2019), a methodology introduced [by myself in 1991](very-deep-learning-1991.html) [\[UN\]\[UN0-3\]](#UN), and later championed by others (2006) [\[UN4\]](#UN4). In fact, it was claimed [\[VID1\]](#VID1)[\[DLP\]](#DLP) that "nobody in their right mind would ever suggest" to apply plain backpropagation to deep NNs. However, in 2010, our team with my outstanding Romanian postdoc Dan Ciresan [showed that deep FNNs can be trained by plain backpropagation and do not at all require unsupervised pre-training for important applications](2010-end-to-end-deep-learning-breakthrough.html) [\[MLP1-3\]](#MLP1)[\[MOST\]](#MOST).
