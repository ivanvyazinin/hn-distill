Protective knitted case for iPod devices

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/IPod_Socks_%286771341%29.jpg/250px-IPod_Socks_%286771341%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:IPod_Socks_\(6771341\).jpg)

iPod Socks in orange and green

**iPod Socks** were a set of multi-colored cotton knit [socks](/wiki/Sock "Sock") introduced by [Apple Inc.](/wiki/Apple_Inc. "Apple Inc.") in November 2004 for protection of [iPods](/wiki/IPod "IPod") from damage during travel.[\[1\]](#cite_note-9to5mac-obscureappleaccessories-1)

## History

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=IPod_Socks&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

The socks were jokingly presented by Apple CEO [Steve Jobs](/wiki/Steve_Jobs "Steve Jobs") as a "revolutionary new product" at a [special music event](/wiki/List_of_Apple_Inc._media_events "List of Apple Inc. media events") held on October 26, 2004.[\[2\]](#cite_note-2)[\[3\]](#cite_note-3) They were available in a package of six different colors, including green, purple, grey, blue, orange and pink, for [US$](/wiki/United_States_dollar "United States dollar")29.[\[4\]](#cite_note-4)

Apple stopped selling the product sometime in September 2012.[\[5\]](#cite_note-ai-apple-removes-ipodsocks-5) The set soon became a collector's item, with aftermarket prices rising as high as [US$](/wiki/United_States_dollar "United States dollar")90 by 2014.[\[6\]](#cite_note-bustle-remember-ipod-accessories-6)

## Reception

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=IPod_Socks&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Reception")\]

Jeremy Horwitz of _iLounge_ gave a rating of B− for the socks, indicating a "Limited Recommendation". Horwitz noted the product's two-toned design and ability to hold an iPod of any size, but criticized the socks for inhibiting access to the screen and controls and high price.[\[7\]](#cite_note-7) In 2021, Chaim Gartenberg of [The Verge](/wiki/The_Verge "The Verge") described the product as a "bizarre piece of Apple's history" comparable to the [Polishing Cloth](/wiki/Apple_Polishing_Cloth "Apple Polishing Cloth"), noting that the socks remained relatively popular during its availability from 2004 to 2012 due to their "relatively universal size" and bright colors.[\[8\]](#cite_note-8)
